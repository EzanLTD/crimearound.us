Filtering your search

CrimeAround.us allows you to filter on different crime report attributes.

Date reported

The date the crime occurred. We have reports going back to Jan 1st, 2001. Be warned, searching over multiple years may take a while to process.

Crime type

Each crime is classified as one of 30 different types, such as Assault, Narcotics, or Prostitution. For more detail, see the full definition list on the Chicago Police Department ClearPath website.

Also, for each incident, multiple offenses can be reported, but the data we get only reveals most serious offense.

We also group crime types in to violent crimes, property crimes and quality of life crimes in the same way that the Chicago Tribune does. In order to accomodate all of the other categories of crime that these three buckets do not cover, we also added an other category.

  • Violent crimes - assault, battery, homicide, robbery, sexual assault
  • Property crimes - arson, burglary, motor vehicle theft, theft
  • Quality of life crimes - criminal damage, narcotics, prostitution
  • Other crimes - criminal trespass, deceptive practice, gambling, interference with a public officer, intimidation, kidnapping, liquor law violation, non-criminal, obscenity, other offense, other narcotic violation, public indecency, public peace violation, ritualism, sex offense, stalking, weapons violation

Advanced filters

Hour of day

We can group crimes by the time of day it happened. Use the slider to select the range of hours that you are interested in.

Location description

Crimes can be filtered by location description as well. This includes places like Liquor stores, CTA stations and streets.

Police beat

The Chicago Police Department divides the city in to 22 districts, which are then divided in to a number of police beats made up of several city blocks. We provide the ability to filter crime by police beat.

Drawing an area to search

Using the tools on the left side of the map, you can draw a custom shape on the map to define the area that you are interested in.


1. Select the shape or box tool on the left side of the map

2. Click on the points on the map to bound your box

3. When done, click the search button and see your results

Remember this search

If you’re interested in saving the results of your search to review at a later time, use the Remember Search link to add a menu item where you can easily reload your results later.

Download data as a spreadsheet (XLS)

If you’re interested in downloading the crime data you’re searched for, you can use the Download link to download the data as an Excel spreadsheet.

Printable PDF

If you’re interested in printing out the results of your search, click the Print link to download a PDF of your search that is suitable for printing.
