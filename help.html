Filtering your search

CrimeAround.us allows you to filter on different crime report attributes.

Date reported

The date the crime occurred. We have reports going back to Jan 1st, 2001. Be warned, searching over multiple years may take a while to process.

Crime type

Each crime is classified as one of 30 different types, such as Assault, Narcotics, or Prostitution. For more detail, see the full definition list on the Chicago Police Department ClearPath website.

Also, for each incident, multiple offenses can be reported, but the data we get only reveals most serious offense.

We also group crime types in to violent crimes, property crimes and quality of life crimes in the same way that the Chicago Tribune does. In order to accomodate all of the other categories of crime that these three buckets do not cover, we also added an other category.

  • Violent crimes - assault, battery, homicide, robbery, sexual assault
  • Property crimes - arson, burglary, motor vehicle theft, theft
  • Quality of life crimes - criminal damage, narcotics, prostitution
  • Other crimes - criminal trespass, deceptive practice, gambling, interference with a public officer, intimidation, kidnapping, liquor law violation, non-criminal, obscenity, other offense, other narcotic violation, public indecency, public peace violation, ritualism, sex offense, stalking, weapons violation
FBI Code Primary Type Description
01A Homicide 1st and 2nd degree The killing of one human being by another.
01B Involuntary manslaughter The killing of another person through negligence.
02 Criminal sexual assault Any sexual act directed against another person, forcibly and/or against that person's will or not forcibly or against the person's will in instances where the victim is incapable of giving consent.
03 Robbery The taking or attempting to take anything of value under confrontational circumstances from the control, custody, or care of another person by force or threat of force or violence and/or by putting the victim in fear of immediate harm.
04A Aggravated assault An unlawful attack by one person upon another wherein the offender displays a weapon in a threatening manner. Placing someone in reasonable apprehension of receiving a battery.
04B Aggravated battery An unlawful attack by one person upon another wherein the offender uses a weapon or the victim suffers obvious severe or aggravated bodily injury involving apparent broken bones, loss of teeth, possible internal injury, severe laceration, or loss of consciousness.
05 Burglary The unlawful entry into a building or other structure with the intent to commit a felony or a theft.
06 Theft The unlawful taking, carrying, leading, or riding away of property from the possession or constructive possession of another person.
07 Motor vehicle theft The theft of a motor vehicle.
08A Simple assault An unlawful physical attack by one person upon another where neither the offender displays a weapon, nor the victim suffers obvious severe or aggravated bodily injury involving apparent broken bones, loss of teeth, possible internal injury, severe laceration, or loss of consciousness.
08B Simple battery A person commits battery if he intentionally or knowingly without legal justification and by any means, (1) causes bodily harm to an individual or (2) makes physical contact of an insulting or provoking nature with an individual.
09 Arson To unlawfully and intentionally damage or attempt to damage any real or personal property by fire or incendiary device.
10 Forgery and counterfeiting The altering, copying, or imitation of something, without authority or right, with the intent to deceive or defraud by passing the copy or thing altered or imitated as that which is original or genuine or the selling, buying, or possession of an altered, copied, or imitated thing with the intent to deceive or defraud.
11 Fraud The intentional perversion of the truth for the purpose of inducing another person or other entity in reliance upon it to part with something of value or to surrender a legal right.
12 Embezzlement The unlawful misappropriation by an offender to his/her own use or purpose of money, property, or some other thing of value entrusted to his/her care, custody, or control.
13 Stolen property Receiving, buying, selling, possessing, concealing, or transporting any property with the knowledge that it has been unlawfully taken, as by Burglary, Embezzlement, Fraud, Larceny, Robbery, etc.
14 Vandalism To willfully or maliciously destroy, damage, deface, or otherwise injure real or personal property without the consent of the owner or the person having custody or control of it.
15 Weapons violation The violation of laws or ordinances prohibiting the manufacture, sale, purchase, transportation, possession, concealment, or use of firearms, cutting instruments, explosives, incendiary devices, or other deadly weapons.
16 Prostitution To unlawfully engage in or promote sexual activities for profit.
17 Criminal sexual abuse The violation of laws prohibiting offenses against chastity, common decency, morals, and the like such as: adultery and fornication; bigamy; indecent exposure; and indecent liberties.
18 Drug abuse The violation of laws prohibiting the production, distribution, and/or use of certain controlled substances and the equipment or devices utilized in their preparation and/or use.
19 Gambling To unlawfully bet or wager money or something else of value; assist, promote, or operate a game of chance for money or some other stake; possess or transmit wagering information; manufacture, sell, purchase, possess, or transport gambling equipment, devices, or goods; or tamper with the outcome of a sporting event or contest to gain a gambling advantage.
20 Offenses against family Unlawful, nonviolent acts by a family member (or legal guardian) that threaten the physical, mental, or economic well-being or morals of another family member and that are not classifiable as other offenses, such as Assault, Incest, Statutory Rape, etc.
22 Liquor license The violation of laws or ordinances prohibiting the manufacture, sale, purchase, transportation, possession, or use of alcoholic beverages.
24 Disorderly conduct Any behavior that tends to disturb the public peace or decorum, scandalize the community, or shock the public sense of morality.
26 Miscellaneous The violation of miscellaneous laws or ordinances.

Advanced filters

Hour of day

We can group crimes by the time of day it happened. Use the slider to select the range of hours that you are interested in.

Location description

Crimes can be filtered by location description as well. This includes places like Liquor stores, CTA stations and streets.

Police beat

The Chicago Police Department divides the city in to 22 districts, which are then divided in to a number of police beats made up of several city blocks. We provide the ability to filter crime by police beat.

Drawing an area to search

Using the tools on the left side of the map, you can draw a custom shape on the map to define the area that you are interested in.


1. Select the shape or box tool on the left side of the map

2. Click on the points on the map to bound your box

3. As soon as your shape is created, you will see your results

Remember this search

If you’re interested in saving the results of your search to review at a later time, use the Remember Search link to add a menu item where you can easily reload your results later.

Download data as a spreadsheet (XLS)

If you’re interested in downloading the crime data you’re searched for, you can use the Download link to download the data as an Excel spreadsheet.

Printable PDF

If you’re interested in printing out the results of your search, click the Print link to download a PDF of your search that is suitable for printing.
